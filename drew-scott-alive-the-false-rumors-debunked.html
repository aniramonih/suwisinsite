FAQs

1. Who is Drew Scott?

Drew Scott is a Canadian real estate expert, entrepreneur, and television personality. He is best known as one of the co-hosts of the popular home improvement and real estate reality TV show “Property Brothers” alongside his twin brother, Jonathan Scott.

Born on April 28, 1978, Drew has become a household name in the world of home renovation and design.

2. What is Drew Scott’s background in real estate?

Before gaining fame as a TV personality, Drew Scott had a strong background in real estate. He studied at the University of Calgary, where he earned a degree in Business Management with a focus on Real Estate.

Drew, along with his brother Jonathan, founded their own real estate investment and development company, Scott Brothers Entertainment. They have successfully flipped properties, turning rundown houses into stunning homes.

3. Is Drew Scott married? Who is his spouse?

Yes, Drew Scott is married. He tied the knot with Linda Phan on May 12, 2018. Linda Phan is a creative director and the couple first met at a Toronto Fashion Week event in 2010.

Their wedding was a highly anticipated event and was even featured in a TV special called “Property Brothers at Home: Drew’s Honeymoon House.”

4. What other TV shows has Drew Scott appeared on?

Apart from “Property Brothers,” Drew Scott has appeared in various television shows and competitions. Some of his notable appearances include “Brother vs. Brother,” where he competes against his brother Jonathan in renovation challenges.

He has also been a contestant on “Dancing with the Stars,” showcasing his dancing skills and entertaining the audience with his performances.

5. Does Drew Scott have any other business ventures?

Yes, Drew Scott is involved in several other business ventures beyond his real estate and television career.

He and his brother Jonathan have their own lifestyle brand called “Scott Living,” which offers a wide range of home furniture, décor, and accessories.

Additionally, Drew is an author and has co-written several books, including “Dream Home” and “It Takes Two: Our Story.” Moreover, he actively engages in philanthropy and is associated with various charitable organizations.
