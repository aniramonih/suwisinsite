Ryan Day is an American football coach and former college football player, presently serving as the 24th coach and head football coach at Ohio State University.

Ryan Patrick Day was born on March 12, 1979, in Manchester, New Hampshire, United States. He was raised by a single mother, Lisa Day following the death of his father, Raymond Day, on January 20, 1988, who died of suicide when he was nine years old.

Ryan Day was raised alongside two younger brothers, Chris and Tim. He attended Manchester Central High School in Manchester, New Hampshire and later proceeded to the University of New Hampshire.

Ryan Day is a married man and had first met his wife, Christina Ourania Spirou (Nina) when they played together on the same tee-ball team when she was seven and he was six. The pair tied the knot in June 2005 and have three children including Ourania, Grace, and Ryan Jr.

Ryan Day Beard

Ryan Day has a tradition for his beard solely on superstition. He spends the offseason growing out a black beard that sits on his face for months as the Buckeyes go through spring football, recruiting and fall camp.

However, when he reaches game week, the beard gets shaven off. He chose to go the clean-faced route during the regular season including his three games as interim head coach in 2018 with the result being a 23–2 record.
