Early Life & Education

Kari Lake, 54 years old, was born on August 23, 1969, in Rock Island, Illinois. She grew up in the eastern part of Iowa, where she was raised by her father, Larry Lake, a high school teacher and football coach, and her mother, Sheila Lake.

Her parents divorced when she was just seven years old. Kari is the youngest among her eight siblings, consisting of seven sisters and one brother.

During her early years, Kari attended North Scott Senior High School, where she laid the foundation for her future success.

Personal Life

Career

Lake’s media career began in the early 1990s when she interned at KWQC-TV in Davenport, Iowa. She then joined WHBF-TV as a daily reporter and weekend weathercaster before moving on to KPNX in Phoenix, Arizona, as a weekend weather anchor.

1998 Lake briefly left Arizona to work for WNYT in Albany, New York, before returning to Arizona in 1999.

Lake became an evening anchor for KSAZ-TV (Fox 10 Phoenix) and remained in this role for over two decades. At KSAZ, she interviewed prominent political figures such as President Barack Obama in 2016 and President Donald Trump in 2020.

However, in 2021, Lake announced her departure from KSAZ shortly before entering the Arizona gubernatorial race.
