Is Robert Smith Gay? What Is the Sexuality of The Cure Band Fame?

Publish date: 2024-08-14

Robert Smith is an English musician. He started the rock band The Cure with two other people in 1978. He is the lead singer, guitarist, and major producer for the band. He is also the only member who has been there since the beginning.

From 1982 to 1984, he was the lead guitarist for the band Siouxsie and the Banshees. In 1983, he was a member of the short-lived band The Glove.

Smith is known for the way he plays the guitar, his unique voice, and the way he dresses. His style, which included pale skin, smeared red lipstick, black eyeliner, a tangled mess of black hair, and all-black clothes, had a big impact on the 1980s goth subculture.

As a member of the Cure, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2019. Rolling Stone magazine named him as the 157th best singer of all time in its 2023 list.

When the band was very famous in France and the United States, people used to think that Robert Smith is gay because the band was so odd compared to others.

So is Robert Smith really gay? Or these rumors are just baseless? Let’s not waste time and dive straight into the article to find out the truth about Robert Smith’s sexuality.

Is Robert Smith Gay?

No, Robert Smith is not gay. He is often mistaken for being gay due to a long-standing rumor that he is in a relationship with his fellow bandmate, Simon Gallup. However, it is important to clarify that Robert Smith is not gay.

In a revealing interview with SPIN in 1987, when asked about the assumption, Robert expressed genuine surprise and responded, “All Americans seem to think you’re gay, Robert. ‘Do they?’ he says, faintly but genuinely surprised:

‘That’s because no one ever sees Mary. We’ve been going together for 13 years—13 years of gayness! She hates having her photograph taken. She hates being associated with me.'”

This statement serves as clear evidence of Robert Smith’s heterosexuality and his long-standing romantic relationship with Mary, debunking any misconceptions about his sexual orientation.

Who Is Robert Smith Married To?

Robert Smith is married to Mary Poole since August 13, 1988. He met her in an acting class at St. Wilfrid’s when he was 14 years old. They don’t have any kids.

According to Contactmusic, Smith said he didn’t want children because he doesn’t like being alive and doesn’t want to make someone else live. Smith also says, “I don’t feel like I’m responsible enough to bring a child into the world.” Smith and Poole have a total of 25 nephews and nieces.

Smith later said that when he first started out in music, Mary didn’t always share his faith and vision for the Cure’s future. This was a big reason why he worked so hard to make sure the band was successful.

The Daily Express said that Mary used to be a model and work as a nurse for children with learning disabilities. However, when the Cure started making more money in the mid-1980s, Mary quit her day job so that she and Robert didn’t have to be apart as much.

Smith told The Face that he had once left a video camera going in their home. “After a couple of hours, you forget it’s on, and I was shocked by how much trash we say to each other,” he said.

It’s like hearing crazy people… I feel more at ease when I’m with people who don’t have their heads on straight because you’re always on the lookout for what they’re going to do next…”

He said that Mary “used to dress up as a witch to scare little children,” that she sometimes dressed up as Robert Smith in his pajamas and that he could never take people home because “I never knew who was going to answer the door.”
