Who is Lisa Franchetti?

Lisa Franchetti is a US Navy admiral. She has been in the news because the Biden administration nominated her for the position of US Navy chief.

She was born in Rochester, New York, on April 25, 1964. After completing her elementary education, she received a Bachelor of Science in journalism from Northwestern University’s Medill School of Journalism in Evanston, Illinois, as well as departmental honors in history while she was a student there.

Later, Lisa Franchetti enrolled in the Navy Reserve Officer Training Corps Program while attending Northwestern, and in 1985, she was commissioned. She also has a master’s degree in organizational management from the University of Phoenix and has attended the Naval War College in Newport, Rhode Island.

Adm. Lisa Franchetti has a distinguished career that encompasses a number of significant accomplishments. She has served in a number of capacities aboard naval warships and onshore, showing her leadership and knowledge in each one.
