Career

Carlos Ghosn worked in Michelin after his graduation in 1978, initially training and working in several plants in France and Germany.  In 1985 he was appointed as a chief operating officer of Michelin’s South American operations. He returned to Rio De Janeiro, reporting directly to Francois Michelin, who tasked Ghosn with turning around the operation, which was unprofitable and struggling under Brazil’s hyperinflation.

In 1989 he was turned the president of Michelin North America. In 1996 he became the vice president in charge of purchasing, advanced research, engineering and development, powertrain operations, and manufacturing in Renault South America division.

In March 1999, Renault and Nissan formed the Renault-Nissan Alliance, and in May 1999, Renault purchased a 36.8 percent share in Nissan. In October 2016, Carlos Ghosn became the chairman of Mitsubishi Motors. They removed Ghosn from his post on 26 November 2018. On 19 October 2018, Tokyo District Prosecutors arrested Ghosn upon his re-entry into Japan. In November 2001, his life story was turned into a superhero comic book series in Japan titled True Stories Of The Carlos Ghosn in Big comic Superior.
