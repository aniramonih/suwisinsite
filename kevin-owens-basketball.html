Kevin Owens is a former professional basketball player who was born on June 9, 1980 in the United States. He played college basketball at the University of Tennessee and was drafted by the Los Angeles Lakers in the second round of the 2002 NBA Draft. He played for the Lakers, the Memphis Grizzlies, the New Orleans Hornets, and the San Antonio Spurs during his professional career. Owens was a two-time All-SEC selection and was named the SEC Player of the Year in 2002. He was also named to the All-Rookie team in 2003. Owens retired from professional basketball in 2008 and is currently a basketball analyst for ESPN. He is also the founder of the Kevin Owens Basketball Academy, which provides basketball training and mentorship to young players. Owens has an estimated net worth of $2 million. He has earned his wealth through his professional basketball career and his work as a basketball analyst.

Kevin Owens (born (1980-06-09 ) June 9, 1980) is an American former professional basketball player. In 2009 Owens played with Sigal Prishtina of the Kosovo Super League. Prior to that Owens played for the Wellington Saints in Wellington, New Zealand and the Ulsan Mobis Phoebus in Ulsan, South Korea of the Korean Basketball League. In the 2006-2007 season Owens played for the Cairns Taipans in Cairns, Australia. Before playing in Australia, Owens played three seasons with the Roanoke Dazzle in the NBA Development League, a minor league system for the National Basketball Association.
