James Hetfield is an American musician, singer, and songwriter who is best known for being the co-founder, lead vocalist, rhythm guitarist, and main songwriter for the famous American heavy metal band, Metallica. Along with the band, he has produced several successful tracks such as Now That We’re Dead, Halo On Fire, Moth into Flame, and many more. He has a huge social media fan base with more than 500k followers on Instagram.

    James Hetfield Facts

  • He grew up with strict parents who were Christian Scientists and did not believe in the use of medicine or any other medical treatment.
  • At the age of 9, he began to take piano lessons, then played drums and at the age of 14, he learned how to play the guitar.
  • His parents got divorced when he was 13 years old and 3 years after their divorce, in 1979, his mother died of cancer.
  • His mother was so loyal to her faith that even after being diagnosed with cancer, she did not take medical help.
  • He has reflected his upbringing and childhood in the songs like Dyers Eve and The God That Failed.
  • After the death of his mother, he went to live with his older half brother.
  • As a teenager, he had been part of a few bands such as Leather Charm and Obsession.
  • James battled alcohol addiction but now maintains complete abstinence as, since 2002, he has been undergoing a rehab program.
  • Some of his ideal interests include hunting, farming, beekeeping, customizing cars and motorcycles in his garage, and going to hot rod shows.
  • He is also an avid sports lover and enjoys baseball, American football, and ice hockey.
  • One of his prized possessions was the 1968 Chevrolet Camaro which was featured in the music video of I Disappear. It was given to him as a gift after the video was shot. James put the car on eBay and the proceeds were given to a Music for Schools program.
  • His first ever signature guitar was by ESP Company, a Japanese guitar manufacturer company.
  • Till now, he has used more than 50 different guitars including ESP Eclipse, Ken Lawrence Doubleneck Explorer, Zemaitis GZ Series GZV500MF-MBK, Gibson Firebird, and many more.
