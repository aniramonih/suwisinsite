Charlie Munger Wife: Nancy Barry And Nancy Huggins

Publish date: 2024-08-15

A well-known and powerful figure in the business and investing world was Charles Thomas Munger. He was renowned for his keen intellect, insight, and command of many academic fields, such as psychology, economics, and history.

Who was Charlie Munger?

Charles Thomas Munger, who lived from January 1, 1924, to November 28, 2023, was a well-known American businessman, investor, and philanthropist. In the business world, he was most notably the vice chairman of Berkshire Hathaway, a conglomerate controlled by Warren Buffett. Indeed, Buffett described Munger as not only his closest partner but also his right-hand man, emphasizing the importance of the two’s collaborative dynamic.

Munger enrolled at the University of Michigan to further his education, concentrating on mathematics. His membership in the Sigma Phi Society during his college years demonstrated his involvement in extracurricular activities. But early in 1943, Munger’s academic career took a strange turn.

He decided to drop out of college and join the US Army Air Corps, where he rose to the rank of second lieutenant, just a few days after turning nineteen. Following his outstanding performance on the Army General Classification Test, he was given orders to attend Caltech in Pasadena, California, to study meteorology. Munger’s relationship with the town that would eventually become his home began with this.

Charles Thomas Munger took advantage of the GI Bill’s educational opportunities by enrolling in a series of advanced courses offered by various universities. His interest in law led him to apply to Harvard Law School, a school associated with his father.

Initially rejected due to the lack of an undergraduate degree, Munger’s admission to Harvard Law was aided by the intervention of Roscoe Pound, a prominent figure in legal education and a family friend. The dean of admissions reconsidered and admitted Munger to the prestigious law school as a result of Pound’s influence.

Meet Nancy Huggins, the first wife of Charlie Munger

Charles Thomas Munger married Nancy Huggins, daughter of Frederick R. and Edith M. Huggins, in 1945 while he was working at Caltech. Nancy, a native of Pasadena, had already made family ties at Scripps College when she lived with Munger’s sister.

Munger and Nancy were married for thirty years. Their three children were Wendy Munger, a corporate lawyer who also served as a trustee at Stanford University and The Huntington Library; Molly Munger, a well-known civil rights lawyer who was a prominent backer of a ballot initiative to raise taxes in California for public education; and Teddy Munger, whose life was tragically taken at the age of nine by leukemia.

Meet Nancy Barry, second wife of Charlie Munger

Charles Thomas Munger remarried a short time later after his first marriage ended. Munger’s union with Nancy Barry, the daughter of Emilie Hevener Barry and David Noble Barry Jr., allowed him to grow his family even more. Charles T. Munger Jr.,

a physicist and Republican activist, Emilie Munger Ogden, Barry A. Munger, and Philip R. Munger are the four children he brought into the family from this marriage. Munger also welcomed two stepchildren, David and William Harold Borthwick, who enhanced the family dynamic.

Munger had to deal with the death of his second wife, Nancy Barry Munger, on February 6, 2010, in the comforts of their own home, at the age of 86.
