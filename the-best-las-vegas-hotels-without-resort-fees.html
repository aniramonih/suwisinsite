The Best Las Vegas Hotels Without Resort Fees

Publish date: 2024-08-11

Las Vegas is a city that never sleeps, filled with exciting attractions, vibrant nightlife, incredible shows, and some of the best hotels in the world. Unfortunately, many of these hotels come with an added charge for resort fees, which can amount to hundreds of dollars during your stay. However, there are a few hidden gems throughout the city that don’t require resort fees, providing an affordable yet luxurious escape for visitors. Here are some of the best Las Vegas hotels without resort fees.

The D Las Vegas

The D Las Vegas is a popular hotel located on Fremont Street in Downtown Las Vegas. This lively hotel is perfect for those looking for a unique and exciting experience, with its vintage Vegas feel. While many hotels in the area charge resort fees, the D Las Vegas doesn’t, making it a great budget-friendly option. The hotel features a variety of amenities, including a casino, rooftop pool, multiple dining options, and a lively bar scene.

The Platinum Hotel and Spa

The Platinum Hotel and Spa is a hidden gem located just off the Las Vegas Strip. This luxurious hotel offers spacious suites with modern amenities, including a full kitchen, living room, and spa-inspired bathroom. The hotel also features a rooftop pool with stunning views of the city, a full-service spa, and a fitness center. While other hotels in the city may charge resort fees, the Platinum Hotel and Spa does not, making it an affordable yet luxurious option for travelers.

The Four Queens Hotel and Casino

The Four Queens Hotel and Casino is another great option for those looking for a budget-friendly hotel without resort fees. Located on Fremont Street in Downtown Las Vegas, this hotel features comfortable rooms with classic Vegas-style decor. Guests can enjoy the hotel’s casino, rooftop pool, and multiple dining options. The Four Queens Hotel and Casino offers an excellent value for travelers looking to experience the excitement of Downtown Las Vegas without breaking the bank.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are resort fees?

Resort fees are an additional charge added to your hotel stay, typically on a daily basis. These fees are charged by most hotels in Las Vegas and cover amenities such as Wi-Fi, fitness center access, and pool access.

2. How much do resort fees typically cost?

Resort fees in Las Vegas can vary depending on the hotel, but they can range from $20 to $45 per night.

3. Why do hotels charge resort fees?

Hotels charge resort fees to cover the cost of amenities that are often included in the room rate, such as Wi-Fi, fitness center access, pool access, and other services. The additional fee allows hotels to offer seemingly lower room rates while still covering their costs.

4. Can I get out of paying resort fees?

Most hotels require guests to pay resort fees, regardless of whether they use the amenities covered by the fee. However, some hotels offer promotions or packages that include resort fees in the room rate.

5. Which Las Vegas hotels don’t charge resort fees?

While many hotels in Las Vegas charge resort fees, some hotels do not, including The D Las Vegas, The Platinum Hotel and Spa, and The Four Queens Hotel and Casino.

6. Are hotels required to disclose resort fees?

Hotels are required to disclose resort fees, but the disclosure may not always be clear. Some hotels may bury the resort fee in the fine print or only disclose it after the guest has booked the room.

7. Can I negotiate the price of resort fees?

It’s unlikely that hotels will negotiate the price of resort fees, as they are often non-negotiable. However, guests can try to negotiate a room rate that includes resort fees in the price to avoid any surprises at check-out.

8. Are resort fees refundable?

Resort fees are typically non-refundable, even if the guest doesn’t use the amenities covered by the fee.

9. How can I avoid resort fees in Las Vegas?

While it may be difficult to avoid resort fees altogether, some hotels offer promotions or packages that include resort fees in the room rate. Guests can also look for hotels that don’t charge resort fees, such as The D Las Vegas, The Platinum Hotel and Spa, and The Four Queens Hotel and Casino.

10. Are resort fees charged at non-gambling hotels in Las Vegas?

Resort fees are typically charged at all hotels in Las Vegas, regardless of whether they have a casino or not.

11. Do all Las Vegas hotels charge resort fees?

While many hotels in Las Vegas charge resort fees, there are some hotels that do not, such as The D Las Vegas, The Platinum Hotel and Spa, and The Four Queens Hotel and Casino.

12. How can I find out if a hotel charges resort fees?

Hotels are required to disclose resort fees, but the information may not always be clear. Guests can check the hotel’s website or contact the hotel directly to find out if they charge resort fees. They can also look for third-party booking sites that disclose resort fees upfront.
