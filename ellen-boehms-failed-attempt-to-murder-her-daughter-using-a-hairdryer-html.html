Ellen Boehms Failed Attempt To Murder Her Daughter Using A Hairdryer

Publish date: 2024-08-16
Spread the love             

Ellen Boehm’s life took a dark turn as she faced the challenges of betrayal, financial struggles, and an obsession with professional wrestling. The events that unfolded, involving the tragic deaths of two of her children and an attempt on her daughter’s life, are a horrifying tale of maternal betrayal.

Affair and Abandonment:

  • Ellen, at 18, entered an affair with Paul Boehm, a married Vietnam War veteran much older than her.
  • They married, had three children, but Paul’s infidelity led to their separation.
  • Paul abandoned Ellen and their children, leaving her in dire financial straits.

Passion for Wrestling:

  • Ellen found solace in her passion for professional wrestling, attending matches and corresponding with wrestlers.
  • Despite financial difficulties, she continued spending on wrestling, exacerbating her family’s financial woes.

Mysterious Death of David Boehm:

  • Ellen’s youngest son, David, died on Thanksgiving Day 1988.
  • Despite attributing the death to Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS), suspicions arose due to odd circumstances.
  • Ellen received a life insurance payout, yet funeral expenses remained unpaid, raising questions.

Attempted Murder of Stacy Boehm:

  • In September 1989, Ellen’s daughter, Stacy, suffered an electric shock in the bathtub.
  • Conflicting stories emerged about how the incident occurred, with suspicions about Ellen’s involvement.

Deceptive Hospital Visit:

  • Ellen planned to take Stacy to a neighbor paramedic but decided to rush her to the hospital.
  • She fed her children a fabricated story about the incident involving a hairdryer.

Tragic Death of Steven Boehm:

  • Just 12 days later, Ellen killed her son Steven by suffocating him with a pillow.
  • She initially blamed vaccinations for his death, adding to the suspicious circumstances.

Ellen’s Confession and Motivation:

  • Ellen confessed to murdering her two sons and attempting to kill her daughter by throwing a hairdryer into the tub.
  • The motive appeared to be financial, linked to life insurance policies she had taken out on her children.

Legal Consequences:

  • In 1993, Ellen Boehm pleaded guilty to first and second-degree murder.
  • She received two life sentences without the possibility of parole.

Ellen’s story is a chilling reminder of the darkness that can lurk within seemingly ordinary lives. Her crimes, fueled by personal struggles and financial motives, left a trail of devastation that shocked those around her. The tragedy serves as a stark illustration of the importance of recognizing signs of distress and seeking help when needed to prevent such heinous acts.
