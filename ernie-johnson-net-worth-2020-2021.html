Career

Ernie Johnson was attending classes at the University of Georgia in 1977, the year in which his career began. He accepted the job as a sports and news director for a radio show, aired at the station by the name of WAGQ-FM. The station was situated in Athens, Georgia.

Ernie stayed at the job for a year or so, until 1978, and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in journalism, at a rank of summa cum laude. He then moved to Macon, Georgia, and started a broadcasting career at the channel WMAZ-TV. He also worked as a news reporter at another channel called WSPA-TV. He joined Turner Sports in 1989.

Ernie Johnson is fondly called as “E.J.” when he covers the studio shows for the coverage of NBA games by TNT. This includes pregame shows, halftime shows, and also postgame show Inside the NBA.
