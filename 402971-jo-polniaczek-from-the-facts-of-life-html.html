  • Four years after her brother's death, the actress showed a rare photo without makeup, not hiding her wrinkles.

Nancy McKeon, born on April 4, 1966, in New York, started her career in entertainment at an exceptionally young age. At merely two years old, she modeled baby clothes for Sears & Roebuck. Her family later moved to Los Angeles after her brother landed a role in the show "Alice."

Life in bustling LA allowed the blooming actress to explore the craft, and by the age of 13, McKeon landed a role in the 1979 TV show, "Stone." This, combined with numerous guest appearances on the hit show "Starsky and Hutch" helped her catch the eyes of the producers of "The Facts of Life."

McKeon's character, Joanna Marie "Jo" Polniaczek, was added to the show in season two and became an irreplaceable part of the project. Following her success in TV, she appeared in several films, including "High School U.S.A.," "Poison Ivy," and "The Puppy's Great Adventure."

She didn't want to be a distant mother because she chose her career over her family, so she receded from the limelight for a while.

The actress also appeared on the Disney channel show, "Sonny with a Chance" and the 27th season of "Dancing with the Stars," alongside the pro-dancer Val Chmerkovskiy. Before the season started, McKeon shared that her two daughters, Aurora Andrus and Harlow Andrus, couldn't wait to see their mom compete.

McKeon, a devoted and loving mother, shares her two children with her husband of 20 years, Marc Andrus. The actress has opened up about being a mom and a wife, revealing it is the role she adores most. She gushed about her kids, saying, "They are two of the bravest little young women I would say that I know."

McKeon and Andrus' paths crossed in 1995 on the set of the Hallmark movie, "A Mother's Gift." The two became close, married in 2003, and had their first baby shortly after.

Nancy McKeon's Brother Suddenly Died Less than Two Months after Their Dad

McKeon wasn't the only famous face in her family. The actress's brother, Philip McKeon, also acted since he was little. The actor became famous for starring in numerous film and TV projects, working alongside industry greats such as Linda Lavin and Charlie Sheen, and many had only good things to say about him.

Sadly, Philip passed away in 2019 after battling an undisclosed illness for a number of years. McKeon opened up about the loss of her beloved brother via a spokesperson, saying Philip's death left the whole family reeling:

"We are all beyond heartbroken and devastated over Phil's passing. His wonderful sense of humor, kindness and loyalty will be remembered by all who crossed his path in life."

A source close to McKeon also spoke out about the actress's emotional state after losing her brother, admitting she was absolutely "devastated."

The siblings were exceptionally close, and the actor had been a vital source of support for his sister after their father, Donald McKeon, passed away a few months earlier.
