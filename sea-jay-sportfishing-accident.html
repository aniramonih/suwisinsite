Sea Jay Sportfishing Accident: A Remarkable Rescue Near Santa Cruz Island

Publish date: 2024-08-11

The Sea Jay sportfishing accident, while undoubtedly a frightening ordeal for all involved, serves as a stark reminder on how anything can go wrong in a millisecond. 

In the early hours of Thursday morning, a sportfishing trip aboard the charter passenger vessel Sea Jay turned into a harrowing experience as the boat struck a rock and began to sink near Santa Cruz Island. 

Thanks to the swift response of the U.S. Coast Guard and a nearby vessel, all 14 people aboard were successfully rescued with no injuries reported.

The incident began at approximately 2:30 a.m. when the Coast Guard Command Center in Long Beach received a mayday call from the captain of the Sea Jay. 

The charter boat, carrying 10 passengers and three crew members, was taking on water due to the impact with a rock.

Faced with the escalating danger, the captain of the vessel made the crucial decision to have everyone abandon the boat.

Quick Thinking and Life-Saving Measures

Before joining the passengers and crew in abandoning the vessel, the captain anchored the Sea Jay and released the life raft. 

Demonstrating his commitment to the safety of everyone aboard, he boarded the raft last in order to ensure the successful evacuation of all aboard.

Upon hearing of the incident, the Coast Guard wasted no time in launching rescue efforts. From Coast Guard Station Channel Islands, a small boat was dispatched, and a helicopter took off from Forward Operating Base Point Mugu. 

Simultaneously, the Coast Guard issued an emergency broadcast, appealing to any nearby vessels for assistance while their crews were en route to the scene.

The emergency broadcast turned out to be a vital component of the rescue operation. The Sea Biscuit, a vessel in the vicinity of the accident, picked up the call for help over the radio and immediately sped to the scene. Once there, the crew of the Sea Biscuit recovered the 10 passengers and 3 crew members from the life raft, undoubtedly providing much-needed relief and assurance to the distressed travelers.

A Cooperative Effort to Ensure a Safe Return

As the Sea Biscuit took on the responsibility of rescuing the stranded party, the California Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW) and the Coast Guard Station Channel Islands deployed a small boat each to assist in transportation and resource allocation. 

Once the Sea Biscuit staff had secured the safety of the passengers and crew, CDFW’s boat and the Coast Guard’s small boat arrived, facilitating a seamless transfer of the survivors to shore.

Upon reaching land, emergency medical services were standing by to attend to any urgent needs, but thankfully, no injuries were reported. 

It is evident that the rapid response and cooperation of various parties – most notably the Sea Biscuit’s crew, the California Department of Fish and Wildlife, and the Coast Guard watch-standers – were crucial in preventing any casualties in this undoubtedly dynamic and high-stakes situation.

A Testament to the Importance of Swift Action and Collaboration

Reflecting on the outcome of the rescue operation, Capt. Ryan Manning, commander of Coast Guard Sector Los Angeles, commended the combined efforts that ensured the successful and safe resolution of the incident. “

This was a dynamic case that resulted in lives saved due to the quick actions of a good Samaritan, our Coast Guard watch-standers, and the California Department of Fish and Wildlife,” he shared.

The Sea Jay sportfishing accident, while undoubtedly a frightening ordeal for all involved, ultimately serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of swift action, preparedness, and teamwork in crisis situations.
