Who is Marilu Henner?

Marilu Henner is an accomplished American actress who was born on April 6, 1952. However, it was in 1978 that Marilu Henner achieved a breakthrough that would define her acting journey. She landed the pivotal role of Elaine O'Connor Nardo in the popular sitcom "Taxi," which aired on both ABC and NBC. Her portrayal of Elaine garnered immense praise and adoration from audiences, leading to a well-deserved five Golden Globe Award nominations.

Amid her successful television career, Marilu Henner also showcased her talent in various films as a co-star. After captivating audiences on the big screen, she returned to television with yet another remarkable achievement. Marilu Henner secured a starring role in the CBS sitcom "Evening Shade," which aired from 1990 to 1994. Her performance in the series further solidified her position as a talented and versatile actress.

Beyond her successful television and film career, Marilu Henner continued to shine with leading roles in numerous television films, displaying her acting prowess and versatility to a broader audience.

Marilu Henner's journey as an American actress has been nothing short of impressive. Her early involvement in "Grease" paved the way for a remarkable career in entertainment.

Marilu Henner Career

Marilu Henner's career has been a diverse and successful one, spanning various entertainment mediums. During her time as a student at the University of Chicago, she made her mark by originating the role of "Marty" in the production of Grease at Kingston Mines in 1971. The show's success led to a move to Broadway, where she was asked to reprise her role, but she opted to join the national touring company, performing alongside John Travolta.

Henner's talent extended to Broadway, where she appeared in numerous productions, including Over Here! with Travolta, revivals of Pal Joey, Chicago, Social Security, and The Tale of the Allergist's Wife.

She ventured into the film industry with her debut in the 1977 sleeper hit Between the Lines, sharing the screen with then-unknown actors like Jeff Goldblum, Lindsay Crouse, John Heard, and Jill Eikenberry. Her second film role came in 1978 opposite Richard Gere in Bloodbrothers.

However, it was her role as Elaine Nardo in the popular situation comedy Taxi that truly brought her to prominence. She portrayed a single mother working as a cabbie with aspirations of becoming an artist. Henner's talent was recognized, and she landed leading roles in several films, such as Hammett in 1982, The Man Who Loved Women in 1983, and Johnny Dangerously in 1984, where she played the love interest of Michael Keaton.

Throughout her career, Henner continued to work with John Travolta, appearing alongside him in the film Perfect in 1985. She earned praise for her performance opposite Steve Martin in L.A. Story in 1991, receiving a nomination for an American Comedy Award as Funniest Supporting Female in a Motion Picture. From 1990 to 1994, she starred opposite Burt Reynolds in the situation comedy Evening Shade.

Apart from her work in film and television, Henner made notable guest appearances on game shows like Match Game and Hollywood Squares. She also lent her voice to the character Veronica Vreeland in Batman: The Animated Series.

Outside of her acting career, Henner pursued other interests. She hosted her own daytime talk show, Marilu, for 165 episodes in 1994. Additionally, she authored several books on diet, health, and memory, with Total Health Makeover being one of the most prominent ones.

Henner's participation in reality television included her appearance on NBC's The Celebrity Apprentice in 2008 and The All-Star Celebrity Apprentice in 2013, winning money for charitable causes. Her exceptional autobiographical memory led her to become a consultant for the CBS drama Unforgettable, where she also guest-starred as a character's aunt.

In 2016, she fulfilled her desire to participate in Dancing with the Stars, being paired with professional dancer Derek Hough. Although they were eliminated in the ninth week, her appearance showcased her dancing skills.

Henner continued her ventures in radio, hosting The Marilu Henner Show, which featured discussions with physicians, health experts, celebrities, and friends. Additionally, she made guest appearances on podcasts and television series like Brooklyn Nine-Nine and The Neighborhood.

Throughout her career, Marilu Henner's passion and talent in various artistic and health-related endeavors have made her a versatile and beloved figure in the entertainment industry.
