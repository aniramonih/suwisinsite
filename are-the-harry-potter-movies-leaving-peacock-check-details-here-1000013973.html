Harry Potter

Harry Potter is a movie based on a book series consisting of seven fantasy novels written by J.K. Rowling, a British author. The novels revolve around the story of a young wizard named Harry Potter, and his two friends, Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, who study at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

The series follows the main story arc of Harry's struggle against Lord Voldemort, a dark wizard who aims to gain immortality and dominate both the Ministry of Magic and all magical and non-magical beings. The series was originally published by Bloomsbury in the United Kingdom and Scholastic Press in the United States. All books around the world are printed by Grafica Veneta in Italy.

The series is a mix of many genres, including fantasy, drama, coming-of-age fiction, and the British school story, which features elements of mystery, thriller, adventure, horror, and romance.

The world of Harry Potter explores various themes and contains many cultural references and meanings. According to Rowling, the main theme of the series is death, while other major themes include prejudice, corruption, and madness.
