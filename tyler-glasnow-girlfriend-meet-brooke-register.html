Tyler Glasnow career

In 2012, Glasnow made his professional debut with the Rookie-level Gulf Coast Pirates, going 0–3 with a 2.10 ERA in 11 games (ten starts). He also made one start for the Class A-Short Season New York–Penn League’s State College Spikes.

He was a player with the Class A South Atlantic League’s West Virginia Power in 2013. During the season, he started 24 games, ending with a 9–3 record, a 2.18 ERA, and 111.1 innings pitched with 164 strikeouts.

His 164 strikeouts in one season surpassed Will Inman’s 134 in 2006 to become the franchise record for the Power. In 2014, Glasnow was a player with the Florida State League’s Class A-Advanced Bradenton Marauders.

After starting 20 games in 2016, Glasnow was with Indianapolis and had a 1.87 ERA with 133 strikeouts in 110+2⁄3 innings thrown.

On July 7, Glasnow was promoted by the Pirates to make his main league debut. In his major league debut, he pitched 5+1⁄3 innings, allowing four runs on three hits and two walks.

He abandoned the game after three innings due to a shoulder ailment in his second start. On July 23, he was added back to the active roster as a relief pitcher. It wasn’t until September 25 that he started another game. With a 4.24 ERA in 23.1 innings pitched, Glasnow was 0-2 for Pittsburgh.

2017 saw Glasnow start in Pittsburgh’s starting lineup. He struggled with command in his first start of the year, pitching 1+2⁄3 innings and surrendering five runs on four hits and five walks.

In exchange for Chris Archer on July 31, 2018, Glasnow, Austin Meadows, and a player to be named later (Shane Baz) were traded to the Tampa Bay Rays.

He was added right away to their starting lineup. With 64 strikeouts in 55+2⁄3 innings, Glasnow recorded an earned run average of 4.20 in his 11 starts with Tampa Bay.

With a 1.75 earned run average and a 5-1 start to the 2019 season, Tyler Glasnow was awarded the American League Pitcher of the Month for the month of April. Following his May 10 arm injury against the Yankees, Glasnow was sent to the 10-Day IL.

With a 4.08 ERA in 2020, Glasnow had a 5-1 record. With seven wild pitches, he tied for the AL lead. During the postseason, both the Blue Jays’ Wild Card round and the Yankees’ Division Series saw Glasnow start the decisive game. He rested for two days before the ALDS Game 5 match.

On June 15, 2021, Glasnow’s right elbow flexor strain and partial ulnar collateral ligament tears were identified. After that, he was put on the 10-day injured list and decided against having surgery in favor of rehabilitation. On June 17, he was placed on the 60-day injury list.

After a botched rehab attempt, Glasnow required Tommy John surgery, it was disclosed on July 31. He might have missed all of 2022 in addition to the remainder of 2021.

The Rays and Glasnow reached an agreement to extend through the 2024 campaign on August 22, 2022. Glasnow was taken off the 60-day IL on September 28.

That evening, Glasnow made his 2022 season debut against the Cleveland Guardians, pitching three innings, striking out three, and giving up one earned run.

In Game 2 of the AL Wild Card Series between the Rays and the Guardians, Glasnow started for the Rays and went five innings without giving up a run while giving up two hits and striking out five.

Oscar González’s walk-off solo home run in the fifteenth inning proved to be the difference between the Rays and the series loss. It was revealed on February 28, 2023, that Glasnow would miss six to eight weeks due to a Grade 2 left oblique strain.
