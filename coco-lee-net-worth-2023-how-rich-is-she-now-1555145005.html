Who is Coco Lee?

Ferren "Coco" Lee, also known as 李玟 (Li Wen) in Chinese, was a renowned Hong Kong musician, actress, dancer, and singer. Born on January 17, 1975, she began her career in Hong Kong and eventually gained recognition in Taiwan and internationally.

Throughout her career, Coco Lee released a total of 18 studio albums, two live albums, and five compilation albums. She made a significant milestone in her musical journey with the release of her first English-language album, titled "Just No Other Way."

One of Coco Lee's notable achievements was her single "Do You Want My Love," which garnered international attention. The song charted in the top 50 of the US Billboard Dance Club Play chart and the Australian Singles Chart, further solidifying her presence in the global music scene.

Coco Lee's talent and popularity transcended music, as she showcased her remarkable vocal abilities by performing "A Love Before Time" from the film Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon at the 73rd Academy Awards. This performance made her the first Chinese American artist to ever grace the stage of the prestigious event.

Beyond her musical accomplishments, Coco Lee was also recognized for her association with the renowned fashion brand Chanel. She proudly served as the first Chinese ambassador for Chanel, highlighting her influence and status within the fashion industry.

Sadly, Ferren "Coco" Lee's life was cut short on July 5, 2023, leaving a significant void in the music and entertainment world. Her contributions to the industry and her trailblazing achievements will always be remembered and cherished by her fans and fellow artists alike.

Coco Lee Early Life and Career

Born as Ferren Lee in Hong Kong on January 17, 1975, Coco Lee moved to California with her family at a young age. She attended Presidio Middle School and Raoul Wallenberg Traditional High School in San Francisco, California.

Around her freshman year at the University of California - Irvine, Lee moved back to Hong Kong to pursue her career in music. She participated in a talent show and placed second, and the very next day, she was offered a recording deal with Capital Artists.

Lee went on to release four albums under Fancy Pie Records before being tapped by Sony Music Entertainment Taiwan in 1996 as their very first artist. She released her debut major studio self-titled album in the same year, which was in Mandarin.

Coco Lee Discography

Coco Lee's music career spans several decades, and she has released numerous albums in Mandarin, Cantonese, and English. Some of her most successful albums include:

  • CoCo Lee (1996)
  • CoCo's Party (1996)
  • Just No Other Way (1999)
  • Exposed (2005)
  • East to West (2010)

Lee's most iconic record is "Do You Want My Love," which was released in 1999 and charted at No. 84 on the UK Singles Chart and No. 49 on Billboard Dance Club Songs.

Coco Lee Acting Career

Apart from her music career, Lee has also dabbled in acting. She made her acting debut in the 1995 film "Whatever Will Be, Will Be" and has since appeared in numerous films and TV shows. Some of her notable roles include:

  • "Master of Everything" (2004)
  • "Kung Fu Dunk" (2008)
  • "The Lady Iron Chef" (2017)
  • "The One" (2020)

Lee's voice acting role in the Mandarin version of "Mulan" and recording the Mandarin version of the hit song "Reflection" also catapulted her to mainstream success.

Coco Lee Personal Life

Coco Lee got married to Canadian businessman Bruce Rockowitz in July 2011, and they have a daughter named Carol. Lee also has two stepdaughters from Rockowitz's previous marriage.

In a statement posted on Instagram by Lee's sister Nancy, it was revealed that Coco Lee had been suffering from depression for a few years and that her condition had deteriorated drastically over the last few months. On July 5, 2023, Coco Lee was revealed to have passed away at 48 years old, following a long battle with depression.
