A Renowned Chef and Television Personality

Mei Lin is a celebrated Chinese-born American chef and television personality. Her claim to fame came with her victory in Bravo’s twelfth season of Top Chef in 2014. Following this triumph, Mei Lin assumed the role of personal chef for media mogul Oprah Winfrey, where she showcased her culinary prowess on a more intimate scale.

Career Highlights

Following her college graduation, Mei Lin embarked on a journey through several esteemed establishments, starting as a cook for the Detroit Lions and later joining Chef Michael Symon’s Roast in Downtown Detroit. Her culinary journey then took her to Wolfgang Puck’s Spago in Las Vegas and Chef Michael Voltaggio’s ink. in Los Angeles. However, it was her notable appearance on Top Chef that served as a pivotal moment in her career trajectory, ultimately leading to her appointment as Oprah Winfrey’s personal chef.

Early Life and Culinary Journey

At 38 years old, Mei Lin’s journey commenced in Guangdong, China, before she relocated to Dearborn, Michigan, at the age of three months. Raised in a culturally diverse environment, her formative years in Michigan greatly influenced her culinary identity. She continued to nurture her culinary aptitude at Schoolcraft College, further refining her skills and knowledge.

Achieving Her Dream

In 2019, Mei Lin realized her long-standing aspiration of owning her own restaurant with the inauguration of Nightshade. Expanding her culinary empire in 2021, Mei Lin introduced Daybird, the inaugural fast-casual Sichuan hot chicken restaurant in the United States. Mei Lin’s culinary finesse shone brightly when she emerged victorious in Food Network’s Tournament of Champions in 2023.
