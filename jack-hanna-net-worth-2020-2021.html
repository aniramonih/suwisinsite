Early Life

John Bushnell “Jack” Hanna was born on 2 January 1947, in Tennessee’s Knoxville. He grew up on the farm of his father.

He completed his schooling in 1965 from Saltsburg, Pennsylvania’s The Kiski School. Following that, he went to Muskingum College for his degree in political science and business.

Jack Hanna has been successfully married to Suzi Egli since 1968. The couple is the parents of daughters named Kathaleen, Julie, and Suzanne. He considers his Labrador as his family member. In 2011, he sparked controversy for defending the order of “shoot to kill.”

Career

Jack Hanna teamed up with his wife to open a petting zoo and pet shop. Later, a boy got mauled by a lion in 1973. Following that, he moved to Florida. From 1973 to 1975, he used to work in Central Florida Zoo and Botanical Gardens, which is a wildlife adventure company.

In 1978, he got an offer from Columbus Zoo. Later, he was named as the zoo’s director. He retired from his post in 1992. In addition to this, he has written many looks like ‘Monkeys on the Interstate,’ ‘Jungle Jack Hanna’s What Zoo-Keepers Do,’ ‘Jungle Jack’s Wackiest, Wildest, Weirdest Animals in the World’, and ‘Jungle Jack: My Wild Life.’

He became the host of the local TV program called ‘Hanna’s Ark’ in 1981. He has also appeared in several TV shows like ‘David Letterman Show,’ ‘The Hollywood Squares,’ ‘Maury,’ ‘Larry King Live,’ ‘Nancy Grace,’ and ‘Good Morning America.’

In 1993, Hanna became the host of the television show ‘Jack Hanna’s Animal Adventures.’ The show successfully went on till 2008. In 2007, he started hosting ‘Jack Hanna’s Into the Wild.’

Hanna has been hosting the TV show ‘Jack Hanna’s Wild Countdown’ since 2011. He has appeared in the music video of “Billy’s Got His Beer Goggles On” by Neal McCoy. Additionally, he has released the album called ‘Jack Hanna’s World’ in 1996 along with Mark Frye
