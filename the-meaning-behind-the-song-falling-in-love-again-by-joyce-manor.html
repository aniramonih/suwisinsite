The Meaning Behind The Song: “Falling in Love Again” by Joyce Manor

As a music journalist, I have come across countless songs that have moved me in various ways. However, few have resonated with me quite like “Falling in Love Again” by Joyce Manor. From the moment I first heard this song, I was captivated by its raw and honest lyrics, which beautifully capture the bittersweet experience of falling in love.

I remember stumbling upon this song at a friend’s house during a gathering. The room was filled with laughter and chatter, but as soon as the opening chords and Barry Johnson’s distinctive vocals began, I found myself completely engrossed. It was as if the song had the power to transport me to a different emotional plane, where vulnerability and nostalgia reigned supreme.

The lyrics of “Falling in Love Again” speak of mixed emotions and uncertainty. The narrator expresses gratitude for someone showing them around and caring for them, but also acknowledges the internal struggle of whether they should feel so deeply for this person. It’s a relatable sentiment that many of us have experienced at some point in our lives.

The chorus, with its simple yet infectious melody, further highlights the complexity of emotions. The narrator finds humor and joy in the person they are falling in love with, as well as their friends. The mention of having money to spend suggests a desire to invest time and effort into this budding relationship. And then, the heartfelt declaration, “I’m falling in love again,” resonates as an admission of vulnerability and a willingness to embrace the uncertain journey of love.

The repeated outro lines, “Then come down to collect it,” offer a sense of closure and reflection. It suggests a realization that the narrator has come to terms with their feelings and is ready to face the consequences, whatever they may be. It’s a poignant reminder that love often involves taking risks and making difficult choices.

Released as part of their album “Never Hungover Again” in 2014, “Falling in Love Again” showcases the signature sound of Joyce Manor. The combination of Chase Knobbe and Barry Johnson’s guitars, along with Johnson’s lead vocals, creates a captivating and emotionally charged musical experience.

In terms of songwriting credits, “Falling in Love Again” is attributed to Chase Knobbe, Matt Ebert, Kurt Walcher, and Barry Johnson. Their collaboration has resulted in a powerful and relatable composition that continues to resonate with listeners.

Listening to “Falling in Love Again” by Joyce Manor invites introspection and tugs at the heartstrings. It serves as a reminder of the universal experiences of love, vulnerability, and self-discovery. Whether you are going through the excitement of falling in love or reflecting on past relationships, this song offers solace in its relatable and honest lyrics.

So, next time you find yourself in need of a musical journey through the complexities of love, give “Falling in Love Again” a listen. Let the emotions wash over you and find solace in the fact that you are not alone in your experiences.
