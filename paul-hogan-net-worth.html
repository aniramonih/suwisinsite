Paul Hogan Overview and Wiki

Paul Hogan, an Australian icon in the entertainment industry, has had a career marked by both remarkable success and notable challenges. Born on October 8, 1939, in Sydney, Australia, Hogan’s journey from a Sydney Harbour Bridge rigger to a celebrated actor and comedian is a tale of talent, timing, and tenacity.

Early Beginnings

Hogan’s first taste of fame came in 1971 when he appeared on the Australian TV talent show New Faces. His unique approach, where he humorously critiqued the judges, captivated the audience and kick-started his career in entertainment.

This led to regular appearances on the news magazine program A Current Affair, where he made humorous comments on various issues.

The Paul Hogan Show

In 1973, Hogan began his own comedy sketch program, The Paul Hogan Show, which he produced and wrote. The show, featuring Hogan’s lighthearted and laddish humor, was popular in Australia, the UK, and South Africa, running for 60 episodes until 1984.

Hogan’s talent was recognized with a TV Week Logie Award for ‘Best New Talent‘ in 1973.

Advertising Success

During the 1970s and 1980s, Hogan became a familiar face in advertising. He promoted Winfield cigarettes and later, the Australian tourism industry. His phrase shrimp on the barbie from a tourism advertisement became particularly famous in the United States.

Crocodile Dundee Series

Hogan’s most significant career breakthrough was the role of Mick Crocodile Dundee in the 1986 film Crocodile Dundee. The film was a massive international success, earning Hogan a Golden Globe for Best Actor – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy.

He also co-wrote the movie, which received nominations for an Academy Award, a BAFTA, and a Saturn Award. Hogan reprised his role in the sequel, Crocodile Dundee II, in 1988.

Other Film Roles

In the 1990s, Hogan starred in Almost an Angel and the Western comedy Lightning Jack. He also appeared in the family-friendly movie Flipper in 1996. In 2001, he returned to his famous role in Crocodile Dundee in Los Angeles.

Later Career

Hogan continued to work in the film industry with roles in Strange Bedfellows (2004) and Charlie & Boots (2009). He also ventured into stand-up comedy, touring Australia with live performances. In 2016, Hogan received the Longford Lyell Award for his contribution to the Australian screen.

Recent Projects

More recently, Hogan starred in The Very Excellent Mr. Dundee (2020), playing a fictionalized version of himself. In 2023, he participated in a live TV roast titled The Roast of Paul Hogan.

Throughout his career, Hogan has been a versatile entertainer, seamlessly transitioning from television to film, and from acting to writing and producing. His work has left an indelible mark on the entertainment industry, both in Australia and internationally.

Personal life

He has been married twice. His first marriage was to Noelene Edwards. They initially married in 1958, divorced in 1981, remarried shortly thereafter, and then divorced again in 1986. This second divorce was notably public and contentious.

Hogan then married his Crocodile Dundee co-star Linda Kozlowski in 1990. The couple remained married until their divorce in 2014. Hogan has five children with his first wife, Noelene Edwards, and one child with his second wife, Linda Kozlowski.
